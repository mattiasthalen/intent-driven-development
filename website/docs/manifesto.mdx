We are building software in an era where code generation is approaching commodity status. AI can write functions, compose components, scaffold entire applications. What it cannot do is decide what should exist, why it matters, and whether the result is right.

The frameworks we inherited — Scrum, Kanban, SAFe — were designed to manage human throughput: how many story points a team could deliver per sprint, how to reduce work-in-progress, how to plan capacity. These were good solutions to the problem of scarce execution bandwidth.

That problem is dissolving. The new bottleneck is judgment, taste, and specification. We need a framework built for that reality.

## Core Beliefs

1. **Intent over implementation.** The highest-leverage act is clearly articulating what should exist and why. Code is a side effect of good intent.
2. **Convergence over velocity.** Speed of typing was never the constraint. Speed of converging on the right answer is everything. We measure how fast we arrive at the right thing, not how much we ship.
3. **Judgment is the product.** Every team member's core output is decisions: what to build, what to skip, what tradeoff to accept, when to stop iterating.
4. **Evaluation over estimation.** We don't estimate how long things take. We evaluate whether what was produced is correct. Story points are replaced by acceptance criteria.
5. **Context is the moat.** AI has infinite capacity but zero institutional memory. The humans hold the domain knowledge, the user empathy, the design taste, and the strategic context. That's the real work.
6. **Small teams, high trust.** When execution is cheap, coordination costs become the dominant expense. Small, high-context teams with overlapping knowledge and minimal handoffs win.
7. **Ship to learn, not to ship.** The goal of every release is to generate information. We ship to get signal, not to hit a date.

## What We Leave Behind

| Old World | Why It Existed | What Replaces It |
|---|---|---|
| Sprint planning | Ration scarce dev capacity | **Intent sessions (continuous)** |
| Story points | Estimate human effort | **Acceptance criteria clarity score** |
| Standup meetings | Sync on blocked work | **Async decision logs** |
| Sprint reviews | Demo completed work | **Evaluation loops (per-change)** |
| Retrospectives | Improve process | **Framework tuning (weekly)** |
| Velocity tracking | Predict team output | **Convergence rate tracking** |
| Backlog grooming | Refine upcoming work | **Intent queue curation** |
| Scrum Master role | Remove blockers, run rituals | **Dissolved into team culture** |
